Education

I graduated from the University of Plymouth in August 2020, achieving a 2:1 in Computing & Games Development. Some of my achievements were creating an anti TD-style VR game known as 'Necromancer Game VR', a 3D, procedurally generated Dungeon Crawler known as 'Dhelve' and a 2D commercial application for Plymouth Energy Community, aimed to show causation between actions a homeowner takes and the effects of damp and mould. I am currently undertaking a part-time masters in Computer Science at Staffordshire University.

Necromancer Game VR

Necromancer Game-VR was a solo project developed in three months using Unity, C# and XML for serialization. It aimed to mix Tower-Defence and RPG elements together within virtual reality to create an immersive experience. As my first foray into VR development, it was used to learn more about emergent technologies and develop my ability to rapidly upskill to meet demands.

Dhelve

Dhelve is a 3D, procedurally generated dungeon crawler built in Unity in a team of two. I dealt with most game systems, including movement, enemy AI and spawning, shop system and player stats.

Damp and Mould Application

The Damp and Mould Application was a program developed for Plymouth Energy Community as a team of three in three months. Built in Unity, It was intended to be used alongside a PEC agent as a visual aid when visiting homeowners.
